# Writing Rules

These are the defaults. CLAUDE.MD's "Reglas de Redacción Propias" extend or override them.
All examples are in Spanish because the reports are written in Spanish.

## Style
- Passive voice for clinical findings: "Se observa…", "Se evidencia…", "Se registra…".
- Refer to the patient by surname in the body ("La paciente García refiere…").
- Professional and precise; no filler or unfounded subjective judgments.

## Terminology
- Full anatomical names; don't abbreviate muscles in the final report
  (in conversation the user may abbreviate; you expand it in the report).
- Kinesiological/functional diagnosis, never medical.
- Expand clinical abbreviations when writing (see `extraccion-datos.md`).

## Units (always explicit)
- Range of motion: degrees (°).
- Strength: Daniels scale 0-5 (e.g. 4/5), with side.
- Pain: EVA 0-10.
- Loads: kg. Distances: cm/m. Time: seg/min.

## Measurements and comparisons
- Every measurement carries a unit and, if applicable, side (derecho/izquierdo/bilateral).
- If there's prior data, present a comparison and describe the trend
  (mejora/estable/retroceso), reading the direction correctly:
  in ROM and strength, more is better; in pain (EVA), less is better.

## What NOT to do
- Don't invent measurements, dates, or diagnoses.
- Don't include medical diagnoses.
- Don't mix data from different patients.
